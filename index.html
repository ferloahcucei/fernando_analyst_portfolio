IMSS Mexico

Expertise tools: Google spreadsheets, Data Cleaning, Tableau.

The IMSS is the institution with the greatest presence in health care and social protection for Mexicans since its foundation in 1943.

The organization of the health care system in the Mexican Institute of Social Security is made up of different levels of care and operation.

First level

Constituted by Family Medicine units and Rural Medical Units, they are attended by specialists in Family Medicine and their function is to provide continuous health care to the individual and their family, with quality, human sense, user satisfaction, as well as with actions of primary and secondary prevention, medical care, limitation of damage and rehabilitation.

Carries out timely epidemiological surveillance actions in the population, strengthens the planning, implementation and control of comprehensive health care programs, promotes health self-care, specific protection, timely detection of diseases, identification and management of factors risky.

Second level

Made up of a network of General Area Hospitals and rural Hospitals that are cared for by specialists in General Surgery, Internal Medicine, Pediatric Gynecology-Obstetrics, Anesthesiology and other specialties, with the functions of: providing true quality medical care with a human sense, to the beneficiaries from the first and third level care units, and those who request it directly in the emergency service.

Third level

Organized by Specialty Hospitals (High Specialty Medical Units), located in Medical Centers and its function is to care for patients with conditions that require highly specialized resources for their management in the medical care units in their area of influence, with technical-medical leadership of the different medical-surgical specialties, in addition to promoting medical education and research events, based on situational diagnosis, to analyze actions that promote the prevention and abatement of health risks.

In the Republic of Mexico, the third level units registered in the open data of the IMSS are 36 (UMAE Medical Unit of specialized attention), in the second level 249 (UMAA Medical Unit of Ambulatory Attention) and in the first level 1,527 (UMF Unit Family Medicine). In total 1,812 health units distributed throughout the country.

World Happiness Score

2015 - 2017

Expertise tools: Google spreadsheets, Data Cleaning, Tableau.

Happiness is an emotional state characterized by feelings of joy, satisfaction, contentment, and fulfillment.

According to The World Happiness Report, a publication of the Sustainable Development Solutions Network.

The subjective well-being measurement is based on three main indicators: life evaluations, positive emotions and negative emotions.

Life evaluations. The global Gallup survey is the source for this data using a 0-10 scale from worst to best. The numerical response on this scale, generally, around 1,000 responses are collected annually for each country.

Positive emotions. Respondents are asked if they smiled or laughed a lot yesterday and if they enjoyed a lot yesterday. If the answer is yes, then it is encoded as 1 and not as 0.

Negative emotions. This is measured by asking respondents if they experienced negative affect for a long time yesterday. Those are worry, sadness and anger.

Comparison of life evaluations provides the most informative measure for international comparisons because they capture quality of life in a more complete and stable way than emotional reports based on daily experiences.

Indicators such as life scale, GDP per capita, Social support, Healthy life expectancy, Freedom to make life choices, Generosity, Perceptions of corruption, Positive affect, Negative affect are considered keys to determine a score in the evaluation of happiness in daily life.

The factors mostly involved in the happiness index are: Family, Life expectancy, Economy and Generosity.

In the following geographic graph it is possible to filter these fields by year.

The countries in strong blue are the happiest, while those that are colored in red are the least happy. In 2017 the 5 happiest countries were Norway, Denmark, Iceland, Switzerland and Finland.

While the 5 least happy are countries like Central African Republic, Burundi, Tanzania, Syria and Rwanda. All in Africa

Data Extraction

Web Scrapping

Expertise tools: Python, Selenium, Pandas, CSV, HTML.

Web scrapping is a method to extract data from a page or website found on the internet, one of my tasks as a data analyst is to obtain data from various sources and then make a union, complement or simply compare with other data. In order to obtain reliable information, sometimes the process must be done by oneself, and in this project, we have a script that allows us to extract the data from a website.


It is possible to apply this method to different scenarios, but in this case, I focus on obtaining the data from a database of soccer matches.

Code in Python



# -*- coding: utf-8 -*-
from selenium import webdriver
from selenium.webdriver.support.ui import Select
from selenium.webdriver.common.by import By
from selenium.webdriver.chrome.service import Service
from time import sleep
import pandas as pd

s=Service('C://Users//benoj//Downloads//chromedriver_win32//chromedriver.exe')
driver = webdriver.Chrome(service=s)
url='https://www.adamchoi.co.uk/teamgoals/detailed'
driver.get(url)


all_matches_button = driver.find_element(By.XPATH, '//label[@analytics-event="All matches"]')
all_matches_button.click()

#Choosing a country value from a select field

select_country = Select(driver.find_element(By.ID,'country'))
select_country.select_by_visible_text('Mexico')

# Select matches but first wait til the table is completely loaded with sleep
sleep(5)

matches = driver.find_elements(By.TAG_NAME, "tr")

print(len(matches))

matches_all = []

for match in matches:
	matches_all.append(match.text)
	
driver.quit()

#Pandas library used to convert the list into a data frame

df = pd.DataFrame({'Matches':matches_all})
print(df)

df.to_csv('matches.csv',index=False)

Computer Vision

Counting people with computer vision

Expertise tools: C++, Visual Studio 2019, OpenCV, Intel Realsense 3D Camera.

Currently we have different methods (mechanical counter, turnstiles, scales, bars with sensors, 2D cameras, among others) to count the people who enter an enclosure (schools, hospitals, theaters, etc.), however, the constant change and advances in technology force us to modify or change these methods to adapt them to the emerging needs. In addition, the change brings with it new requirements and new specifications, so this project consists of a system capable of count people by computer vision (using a 3D camera) who come in and leave, with the objective of determining the number of people who are within an enclosure.

3D Camera Intel RealSense Model D415

The 3D images are data maps that contain the RGB color plane and also one that is a matrix of depth data. These data are essential to calculate the distance at which the objects are. Said matrix is obtained from an infrared ray that is emitted by one of the components of the camera. The time it takes for the beam from exiting to returning to the camera lens (a kind of bounce) is what a distance calculation provides.

Figure shows a 3D Image viewer pointing to a desk and a computer.

The 3D image processing makes it possible to identify, in this case, the bodies that approach a coded threshold in order to delimit an access area and, in this case, the passage of people in a bidirectional way.

The algorithm to carry out the counting of people is based on the delimitation of areas of interest (ROI), these are extracted from the acquired image after image processing. Said processing performs a series of actions to adapt the image, which are detailed in the following table.
